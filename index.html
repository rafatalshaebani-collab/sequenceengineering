Practice 02 — Data Centres

The technical delivery partner for mission-critical data centres.

We sit alongside your development, design, construction, commissioning and operations teams — providing the technical leadership and interface management to deliver an entire data centre project lifecycle, from site selection to steady-state operations.

Why Sequence

Mission-critical projects fail at the interfaces — not the systems.

Individual systems usually work. Programmes slip and facilities underperform in the gaps between them — between owner and EPC, design and construction, vendors and operations, and across the controls platforms that bind a building together. We provide the structured technical management and interface control that holds a complex, multi-party build together — and we can join at any point on the line.

Across the whole lifecycle

From site selection and development through design, construction, commissioning, handover and operations — one accountable technical partner, not a single-discipline consultant who hands the problem on.

Owner-side and interface-first

We represent your technical interests, coordinate consultants, vendors and contractors, and actively manage the interfaces where mission-critical projects actually break.

Operator-grade, controls-deep

Built by engineers who have commissioned and operated mission-critical and utility-scale assets, with real depth in SCADA, EPMS, BMS and DCIM — where many firms are weakest.

Data Centre Delivery

Technical support across every stage of the project lifecycle.

A complete delivery capability for purpose-built, hyperscale and AI/HPC facilities. Engage us for a single stage, or keep us alongside your team from first concept through to long-run operations.

01 — Inception

Development & project inception

Most engineering firms start at design. We start earlier — de-risking the asset while the decisions that govern cost, schedule and capacity are still being made.

Development support

Technical due diligence, site assessments, utility engagement, grid-connection coordination, capacity planning, and infrastructure & expansion strategy.

Owner’s representation

Technical governance, consultant coordination, stakeholder management, design oversight, contractor engagement and development risk management.

Early contractor engagement

Procurement strategy, tender support, technical evaluations, and scope & package definition before commitments are locked in.

02 — Design

Design management

Coordinating multiple engineering disciplines and stakeholders into an integrated, constructible and operationally reliable facility — not a stack of disconnected design packages.

Design management

Design reviews, design assurance and governance, value engineering and design risk reviews held to the standard the facility will be operated to.

Multi-discipline coordination

Electrical, mechanical, civil, structural, fire, security, communications and controls — coordinated as one system, not in silos.

Design integration

Clash identification, interface management, design change management, constructability and operational readiness reviews.

03 — Interfaces

Interface management & systems integration

The discipline most often missing on complex builds — structured management of the boundaries between every party, system and discipline.

Electrical interfaces

Utility connection, HV and MV systems, UPS systems and generator systems — coordinated end to end.

Mechanical interfaces

Cooling systems, chilled-water systems and building management systems, including high-density and liquid-cooling readiness.

Integration management

Interface registers, design integration workshops, interface tracking, verification and risk management.

Construction management & delivery
